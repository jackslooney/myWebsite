    • Ookla the Brave

    Background

    From the start, Ookla has always been an outcast. As the son of the village healer, he had little status in a social ladder based on physical prowess. However, as Ookla developed and become an Orcish teen, it became clear that he was blessed with a warrior's body, rather than the meek body of healer (along with the brains of one). Because of the nature of predjudice, Ookla was still scorned by both man and orc alike. This made him grow up fast, with all of the skills to survive in the wild, as he wasn't allowed to interact with the "normal villagers". One day, he came back to his mother's hut to see the village chief standing over his mother's broken body. Using his superior combat ability, he bested the chief in honorable combat, but, even though it was a legal bout, he was chased out of the village, and is hunted by the village warriors to this day. So far, Ookla's superior survival and combat skills has allowed him to stay a few steps ahead of them, but who knows how long that will last...

    Skills

    Ookla's main quality is his indominable strength. He has no trouble with almost anything strength based. Need someone to tow a whole sledful of people? He's your man! Need to throw a party member over 100 feet for a contest? No problem.

    Ookla, being from a relatively small orcish village, doesn't speak common, and as a result sometimes has a hard time communicating with others. He may come off as unintelligent, but don't be fooled, he is no simpleton. When talking through an interpreter, he can be very refined.

    Admonisher

    Following the party's encounter with Krampus, Ookla recieved an incredible Axe from Gond, the god of artificy.

    1. +2 to all attack and damage rolls
    2. Crystalline Song: Each hit can deal an additional 1d8 thunder damage
    3. Momento of the fallen: the voices of the souls whose lives this legendary axe have taken can be heard within the head of the axe. The weilder can conjure the image of that soul's death, as well as make those nearby hear those voices.
